About Vidyasagar University

Vidyasagar University, established in 1981 by the Government of West Bengal and named after the eminent educationist and social reformer Pandit Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar, is a leading public university committed to academic excellence, inclusivity, and social transformation. Located in Midnapore town, Paschim Medinipur, West Bengal, the University serves a diverse student population, with a strong focus on learners from rural, tribal, and economically disadvantaged backgrounds. The University comprises 33 academic departments and offers 36 postgraduate programmes and 27 Ph.D. programmes across Arts, Science, Commerce, Management, and interdisciplinary studies, with curricula aligned to the National Education Policy, including the Multiple Entry and Exit system, promotion of Indian languages, and integration of the Indian Knowledge System. As an affiliating university, Vidyasagar University plays a pivotal role in strengthening higher education across the region while maintaining rigorous academic standards.

The University has developed a robust research and innovation ecosystem, hosting over 750 Ph.D. scholars (full-time and part-time) and awarding more than 350 doctoral degrees in the last three years. Faculty members and researchers actively engage in sponsored research, consultancy, and patent-driven innovation, with 50 funded research projects in 2023–24 alone. Continuous investment in modern infrastructure including advanced laboratories, libraries, engineering workshops, ICT-enabled classrooms, and research facilities is supported by an annual operational expenditure exceeding ₹47 crore. Accredited by NAAC with validity up to 2026, the University has also earned national recognition, being placed in the 151–200 band in the NIRF Overall category and in the 51–100 band among State Public Universities.

Committed to sustainability and inclusivity, over 80% of campus buildings are equipped with ramps and lifts, alongside initiatives such as solar energy utilization, rainwater harvesting, plastic-free practices, and green campus policies. With its learner-centric academic environment, experienced faculty, vibrant campus life, and emphasis on research, employability, and ethical values, Vidyasagar University continues to nurture confident, skilled, and socially responsible graduates prepared to contribute meaningfully at national and global levels.

About the Conference

The School of Advanced Sciences (SAS) at VIT Chennai, in prestigious collaboration with the Department of Applied Mathematics at Vidyasagar University, is proud to organize the International Conference on Graphs and Fuzzy Graphs (ICGFG-2026) in coordination with the Sridharacharya Academy of Mathematical Sciences (SAAMS). This landmark event commemorates the 50th Anniversary of Fuzzy Graph Theory (1975–2025). ICGFG-2026 aims to provide a premier global stage for researchers, academicians, and practitioners to exchange pioneering ideas and discuss the latest breakthroughs in graph theory, fuzzy sets, and fuzzy graphs.

A Platform for Innovation

ICGFG-2026 serves as a dynamic forum for exploring theoretical advancements and emerging practical applications. The conference highlights the intersection of mathematical theory with modern Technology, focusing on fields such as:

  • Artificial Intelligence & Data Science
  • Optimization & Communication Networks
  • Decision-Making Systems

Hosted at the vibrant VIT Chennai Campus, the event will feature a comprehensive program including keynote lectures, invited talks, and Technical sessions presented by leading global experts.

The conference focuses on, but is not limited to:

  • Graph Theory
  • Fuzzy Set Theory
  • Fuzzy Graphs and Applications
  • Algorithms and Optimization
  • Artificial Intelligence and Data Science
  • Network Science and Communication Systems
  • Decision-Making Models and Soft Computing Techniques

Who Can Attend?

ICGFG-2026 is an inclusive platform designed for anyone involved in the mathematical and computational sciences:

✓Academicians and Faculty: To share pedagogical insights and research breakthroughs.

✓ Research Scholars (Ph.D./Post-doc): To present their work and expand their professional network.

✓ Undergraduate and Postgraduate Students: To learn about emerging trends and identify potential research areas.

✓ Industry Professionals: To explore how fuzzy graph models can be applied to optimization, logistics, and AI systems.

✓ Data Scientists and Engineers: To leverage graph-based algorithms for complex network analysis.
